Notes on Generalization under infinite width regimes

A short(ish) report on a few ways generalization bounds have been computed under a 2-layer ReLU neural network under the infinite width limit, and the Neural Tangent Kernel Parameterization (lazy-regime). Some discussion of works that use the mean-field parameterization towards the end.
Generalization under infinite width limitsPrajjwal Bhattarai pb2276@nyu.eduAbstractIn this project, we aim to survey a few recent papers that aim to study the generalizationdynamics of overparameterized neural networks. We study the problem of estimating thebound on population loss on a two-layer ReLU neural network, optimizing using gradientdescent under the Neural Tangent Kernel parameterization. We analyze a O( 1√n ) boundin a noiseless setting (i.e. when responses are deterministic functions of inputs), and showthat in the presence of random noise, the bound becomes vacuous.1 IntroductionIn an era of larger models achieving performance improvements over a generalized set oftask, the theory behind understanding their impressive generalization capabilities is lim-ited. Traditional optimization and learning theory does not always successful predict theperformance of neural network, while theoretical analysis of deep neural networks, as theyare used in practice, are restricted by their non-convex properties and overparameteriza-tion. Some reductions have been proposed in deep learning theory to make analysis moretractable. A useful model has been the linearized dynamics of a two layer ReLU networktrained with gradient descent parameterized with the so-called lazy training parameteri-zation. When the width of the network tends to infinity, the neural network essentiallybecomes a kernel regressor, with a kernel that has been termed as Neural Tangent Kernel(NTK). Understanding the dynamics of a neural network within this parameterization ismuch easier, and some work have attempted to understand the generalization properties oflarge neural networks under this regime. There is still an empirical gap between the pro-posed theoretical bounds and empirical performance, and the questions of whether classicalstatistical notions of generalization are still applicable in cases of large language models areoften raised.
